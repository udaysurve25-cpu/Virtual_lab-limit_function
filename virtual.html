Learning Objectives

The primary aim of this module is to provide students with a solid understanding of limits and functions, which serve as the foundation for differential and integral calculus.

By the end of this module, students should be able to:

  • Understand the concept of a limit and its notation
  • Evaluate limits of functions algebraically and graphically
  • Identify different types of discontinuities in functions
  • Apply the concept of limits to determine function continuity
  • Understand one-sided limits and their significance
  • Apply limit laws to evaluate complex limits
  • Use the Squeeze Theorem to evaluate certain limits
  • Understand limits at infinity and infinite limits

This module combines theoretical explanations with interactive simulations to cater to different learning styles and help build intuition about these important mathematical concepts.

Theoretical Foundations

The concept of a limit is fundamental to calculus and mathematical analysis. It describes the behavior of a function as its argument approaches a particular value.

Definition of a Limit

Formally, we say that the limit of f(x) as x approaches a is L, written as:

limx→a f(x) = L

if for every ε > 0, there exists a δ > 0 such that if 0 < |x - a| < δ, then |f(x) - L| < ε.

Types of Limits

  • Two-sided limits: The function approaches the same value from both sides
  • One-sided limits: Limits approached from either the left (x→a⁻) or right (x→a⁺)
  • Infinite limits: The function increases or decreases without bound
  • Limits at infinity: The behavior of a function as x approaches ∞ or -∞

Continuity

A function f is continuous at a point a if:

  1. f(a) is defined
  2. limx→a f(x) exists
  3. limx→a f(x) = f(a)

Understanding these concepts is crucial for further study in calculus, including derivatives and integrals.
